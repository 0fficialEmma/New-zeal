BENEFITS OF A GRATEFUL HEART:
MERCY

LESSON 40

2ND JUNE

BIBLE TEXT

Psalm 51

MEMORY VERSE

Ps 145:9-10 - "The LORD is good to all; and his tender mercies are over all his works. All thy works shall praise thee, O LORD; and thy saints shall bless thee."

CENTRAL TRUTH

The grateful heart attracts the mercy of God.

INTRODUCTION

Mercy is defined as the compassionate treatment of those in need,
especially when it is within one's power to punish or help them. The person showing
mercy is at an advantaged position. He has what the other person does not have. The
Bible says God showed us mercy when we did not deserve it. Mercy is when we receive
what we do not deserve. When we think of the goodness of God, we are able to see our
frailty and how we have not measured up to His standard. Psalm 51 is an expression of
repentance and mercy.

CLASS ACTIVITY: Differentiate between mercy and pity.

LIFE APPLICATION: Write out how you have offended God and consider the
punishment or judgement that should have followed, had He not shown you mercy.
You are saved by His mercy. He forgives your sins by His mercy.

CONCLUSION: As much as it pleases God to show mercy to us despite our
unfaithfulness, He expects that we respond with gratitude. God forgives us when we sin,
withholds punishment that we deserve and shows us mercy. When we think of the
goodness of God and all He has done for us, we give God thanks continuously.
